万物有灵 Animism
剪纸艺术起源于人类对大自然的敬畏和顶礼膜拜。远古时候，我们的祖先认为世界上一切不受人的身体直接控制、不受人的意愿随意支配的万事万物，都各自受着某种神灵的驱使。于是，就有了很多的神灵，如日神、月神、土地神、山神、河神等等。他们也因此形成了一种“万物有灵”的神灵观念，即鬼神观念，并在剪纸艺术中体现了出来。
The art of Chinese paper cutting originated from the worship of human beings to nature. In ancient times, our ancestors believed that all things in the world that are not directly controlled by the human body or human will, are driven by some kind of god. As a result, there are many gods, such as sun gods, moon gods, earth gods, mountain gods, river gods and so on. They also developed the concept of "animism”, that is, the concept of ghosts and gods, which was reflected in the art of paper-cutting.
意象 Imagery
神仙、瑞龙、祥云、飞鸟、人物、走兽、植物等纹样皆是”以象寓意，以意构象“，象征着中国人民千百年来追求吉祥的永恒主题。过年贴的窗花纹样以简洁的线条再现远古图腾、巫术崇拜、民俗文化的具象，也代表了那个时代人们对于审美的追求。
Immortals, auspicious dragons, auspicious clouds, birds, figures, animals, plants and other patterns are all "symbolized with images, and constructed with symbols", representing the eternal theme of the Chinese people's pursuit of auspiciousness for thousands of years. The window patterns pasted on the New Year's Eve reproduce the representation of ancient totems, witchcraft and folk culture with simple lines, also representing people's pursuit of aesthetics in that era.

守门娃娃 Gatekeeper Doll
在陕西一些较为偏僻的农村，一些源于原始巫术的习俗尚有孑遗。在陕北，“瓜籽娃娃”、“守门娃娃”在春节时几乎每家都贴，以用来防病魔和邪气入室。
In some remote villages in Shaanxi, there are still remnants of some customs originating from primitive witchcraft. In northern Shaanxi, "Melon Seed Dolls" and "Gatekeeper Dolls" are posted in almost every house during the Spring Festival to prevent diseases and evil spirits from entering the house.
门神 Door God
门神剪纸经常被用于吓走妖魔鬼怪。在延续祭门礼俗文化同时，它也逐渐发展成较为系统且颇具群体性的民间艺术门类。它具有深厚文化内涵的载体，传递着辟邪禳灾的文化意蕴以及人们的自然崇拜和神灵信仰观念，充分展现了农耕文化下的原始意识。
Door god paper-cuts are often used to scare away demons. While continuing the ritual of worshiping gates, it has gradually developed into a relatively systematic and collective category of folk art. It has a carrier with profound cultural connotations of warding off evil spirits and disasters, as well as people's concept of nature worship and belief in gods, fully demonstrating the original consciousness under the farming culture.
老虎和金鸡 Tigers and Golden Roosters
由于对原始图腾的崇拜，一些动物常被看成具有震慑和压服邪祟神力的非凡之物而用作驱鬼避邪。比如在陕北人眼里，老虎和金鸡是鬼最恐惧的，因此把作为百兽之王的老虎和有神职功能的金鸡视为他们的保护神。春节时常常会剪一对辟邪虎或金鸡贴于门上把守家门，震慑鬼魅，恶鬼惧而远之。生命、灵魂与剪纸的深厚渊源，折射出人民群众追求美好生活的淳朴民族情怀。
Due to the worship of primitive totems, some animals are often regarded as extraordinary things with the power to subdue evil spirits and are used to exorcise and ward off evil spirits. For example, in the eyes of people in northern Shaanxi, tigers and golden roosters are the most feared by ghosts, so they regard the tiger, the king of beasts, and the golden rooster, the role of priesthood, as their gods of protection. During the Spring Festival, a pair of evil-proof tigers or golden roosters are usually cut and attached to the door to guard the house, so as to frighten the ghosts. The deep connection between life, soul and paper-cutting reflects the simple and honest national sentiment of the people in pursuit of a better life.
剪纸纹样 Textures
剪纸自己的语言主要是通过剪纸纹样体现出来的，常用的有月牙纹，锯齿纹、云纹、柳叶纹、火纹、水纹等，人们用这种独特号语言作为装饰，使剪纸图案内容更为丰富，形式更多变。通常一幅作品中会运用多种装饰纹样，其中也蕴含了“一成万物，天地同系”，与自然和谐共存的哲思。
The language of Chinese paper cutting is mainly reflected through the patterns of paper-cutting. Commonly used are crescents, serrations, moirés, willows, fires, waters, etc. People use this unique language as decoration to make the content of paper-cut patterns richer and more varied. Usually, a variety of decorative patterns are used in one piece of work, which also contains the philosophical thinking of coexisting in harmony with nature, because "all things live together, heaven and earth are destined".
锯齿纹 Serrations
锯齿纹俗称“打⽑刺”，它是剪纸艺术中最典型、最有代表性也是难度最⼤的技法之⼀，精致的打⽑宜剪不宜刻。它通常⽤于表现动物⾝上柔软的绒⽑、⽻⽑，动物的牙齿，鱼的鳞片，花瓣的边缘以及树叶的纹理等。锯齿纹以长短、疏密、曲直、刚柔表达丰富的韵律感和节奏感，也能更好的表现物象的层次感和⽴体感。
Serrations are commonly known as "thorns". It is one of the most typical, representative and most difficult techniques in the art of paper-cutting. Delicate thorns should be cut rather than carved. It is usually used to express the soft fleece and hair on animals, the teeth of animals, the scales of fish, the edges of petals and the texture of leaves. Serrations express a rich sense of rhythm with length, density, straightness and rigidity, and can also better express the sense of layering and volume of objects.
月牙纹 Crescents
月牙纹在剪纸作品中应用亦十分广泛，如动物、人物的眉眼，花饰和植物动物的弧度修饰等。有的作品甚至可以多处采用月牙纹装饰，因为它可长可短、可宽可窄、可曲可直。同时，月牙纹还可以变换为云纹、水纹和动物身上的旋转花纹等。
Crescents also widely used in Chinese paper cutting, such as the eyebrows and eyes of animals and figures, flower decorations and the curvature modification of plants and animals. Some works can even be decorated with crescents in multiple places, because it can be long or short, wide or narrow, curved or straight. At the same time, the crescent pattern can also be transformed into moiré, water pattern and rotating patterns on animals.
冒铰与团花 Maojiao and Tuanhua
冒铰指不打草稿，拿起剪刀红纸随意地剪，在团花剪纸中最常见。团花是中国剪纸历史最悠久、运用率最广泛的一种形式。它的关键在于掌握好几种不同的折叠方法，然后不经意地剪上几剪刀。每个图形自成单元，相互勾连，展开后便得到一幅均齐的、呈辐射状的团花了。作为折叠剪纸，它最能体现剪纸窗花多次折叠、重复造型的优势。经过几番折叠的纸上剪好平展开来，意想不到的奇迹之花就在你手中绽放。
Maojiao(冒铰) refers to picking up scissors and cutting the red paper at will without making a draft, which is the most common in Tuanhua(团花) paper-cutting. Tuanhua is the oldest and most widely used form in Chinese paper cutting. It's all about mastering several different folding methods and then inadvertently cutting a few. Each figure forms its own unit and is linked to each other. After unfolding, a uniform and radial Tuanhua is obtained. As a folding paper-cut, it can best reflect the advantages of multiple folding and repeated shapes of paper-cuts. After several times of folding the paper, cut it flat and unfold it, and the unexpected miracle flower will bloom in your hands.

祝由之术 The Art of Zhuyou
古代的一种精神疗法。治疗小儿腹泻 “剪作花，贴眉心” 治疗难缠危急的产妇 “纸花剪如杏叶大，摊上贴脐心”。
An ancient spiritual therapy. For example, to treat children with diarrhea, cut paper into flowers and stick them between children’s eyebrows; to treat women having a difficult childbirth, cut paper the size of an apricot leaf and spread it over their belly buttons.
